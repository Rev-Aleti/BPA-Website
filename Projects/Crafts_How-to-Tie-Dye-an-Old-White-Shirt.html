How to Tie Dye an Old White Shirt

Materials:
  • Borax
  • Water
  • Rubber Bands
  • Rubber Gloves
  • Squeeze Bottles
  • Soda Ash
  • Large Ziplock Bags
  • Tub
  • Wooden Dowel/Rod
  1. Mix the soda ash with warm water in the tub. Then soak the shirt in this mixture for 10 minutes.
  2. Wring out the shirt and lay it down flat. Now put the wooden dowel on the middle of the back of the shirt. Turn the shirt until it is at a pie like shape.
  3. Put multiple rubber bands on the shirt while maintaining its flat pie shape.
  4. Put on your Rubber Gloves it will get quite messy in the next few steps.
  5. Now simply put the Procion Dye over certain parts of the shirt. The more colors you use, the better the final product.
  6. Now flip the shirt over and repeat step 5.
  7. Put the shirt in a Ziplock Bag and wait for 24 hours. Once you take the shirt out and remove the rubber bands, you should have a tie dye shirt!
