Make a Silver Ring for 25 Cents

Materials:
  • Silver Quarter
  • Spoon
  • Drill
  • Metal File
  • Vice or Vice Grips
  1. Find a silver quarter. Any quarter from after 1964 should be silver.
  2. Hold the coin in one hand and tap the edge with a spoon This is a tedious and long process. A hammer can help speed up the process; however, the finished ring will look rough.
  3. Determine the size of your finger. Tap the ring until the size is within 2mm of the desired size.
  4. Next, make a hole. You need to hold the ring in something that won't scratch it. A vice or vice grips will work. What ever you use, make sure you pad it to protect the ring. You don't want all that tapping to go to waste. **Also note that drilling the ring gets it very hot. It can burn you, handle with care**. Drill a hole in the center and work your way out. A file will probably work well here. Once you have enough of the center out you can grind away the edges with a file or dremel. At this point you can play around with the size. Try the ring on and grind away until it fits right.
  5. Polish your new ring! The file can be used to clean out rough spots inside the ring.
  6. *WARNING: Check your state or nation laws on defacing money before starting this project.* In the United States, you can alter coins into jewelry, but it cannot be claimed to be greater than its original value.
